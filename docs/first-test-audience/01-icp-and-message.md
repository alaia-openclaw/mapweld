# ICP and Messaging Boundaries

This document locks the first-test audience profile and approved messaging for MapWeld outreach.

## First-test ICP

- Company type: fabrication shops and modular piping teams with mixed roles (shop, QA/QC, office).
- Team shape: 3-20 people touching weld records and status handover.
- Work pattern: uses PDF isometrics plus spreadsheets or paper for traceability.
- Activity threshold: at least 10 weld records per week on active jobs.
- Trigger pain:
  - tracing weld status across multiple files and chats;
  - retyping field notes into office spreadsheets;
  - handover friction from missing links or late corrections.

## Buyer and User Roles

- Champion user: QA/QC lead or documentation owner.
- Daily users: shop supervisor/foreman, inspector, document control.
- Economic approver (early): operations manager or project manager.

## Approved Positioning

- Primary message: weld traceability stays on the drawing, not in disconnected sheets.
- Product truth:
  - browser-based workflow on PDF isometrics;
  - portable `.weldproject` save/load;
  - Excel export for office handover;
  - optional health checks for consistency before turnover.
- Proof angle: no install, no account, no backend setup required for MVP usage.

## Claims to Avoid

- Do not claim multi-user real-time collaboration.
- Do not claim cloud synchronization or centralized server.
- Do not claim authentication/permissions model.
- Do not claim full databook compilation as generally available MVP.

## Offer for First Test Audience

- 30-day guided pilot on one active project.
- One named champion and one weekly 20-minute feedback call.
- Success condition: team can run the workflow without live support by week 2.

## Qualification Checklist

- Must have a current project drawing in PDF.
- Must have one person responsible for weld register output.
- Must agree to anonymized feedback capture and usage metrics.
- Must have at least one weekly pilot review slot.
